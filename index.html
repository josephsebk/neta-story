The Spark

Yesterday, on an investing focused WhatsApp group I am on, someone posted this:

A joke about political parties being the only "meaningful growth avenue" in India. The laughing emojis piled up. But it got me thinking.

The Vibecoding

So I did some vibecoding to parse all the MPs' financial disclosures.

We built a pipeline to extract every stock and mutual fund declared in the official affidavits of 543 Lok Sabha 2024 candidates. Matched them to live market tickers. Reconstructed 104 complete portfolios.

Note: Massive promoter holdings — like Apollo Hospitals (Rs 2,500+ Cr), Jindal Steel, and IMFA — are excluded from discretionary rankings.

The Discovery

What happened next will...

...overturn assumptions about how politicians invest. Stripped of dominant promoter stakes, their discretionary portfolios still outperformed the broader index. Their spouses did even better.

Let's look at the numbers.

Chapter 1

MPs vs. the broader market.

104 reconstructed political portfolios, benchmarked against the Nifty 50 over a two-year tracking window from the nomination period (May 2024) to today.

Aggregate Returns (%)

Key finding: The Nifty 50 returned a moderate +3.63%. MPs' discretionary portfolios returned an asset-weighted +6.48%. Their simple average was +7.92% — nearly double the benchmark. 63.5% of trackable portfolios beat Nifty.

Including family promoter holdings (Apollo, Jindal, IMFA), the total combined political assets index grew by +38.71% — from Rs 2,919 Cr to Rs 4,050 Cr.
